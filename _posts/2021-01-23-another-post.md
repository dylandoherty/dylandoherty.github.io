Most defenses of voting attempt to show that despite the miniscule chance that your vote will affect the outcome of an election, the states are so high that doing so still has positive expected utility.

I want to suggest another alternative, from above, so to speak. The reason we ought to vote is because we want out candidate to win. Specificially, we are guided by the following principle:

1) Get as many people to vote for your candidate as possible.

This is why you talk about your candidate and offer to drive voters to the polls and volunteer for candidates. Your goal is to get others to vote, as many as you can. By failing to vote oneself, one violates this principle.

To be clear, there is nothing here about the causal relationship between one's vote and one's candidate winning.
